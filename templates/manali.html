The Valley of Gods

History of Manali

Manali, a beautiful hill station in Himachal Pradesh, is known as the "Valley of Gods". According to Hindu mythology, the name "Manali" is derived from 'Manu-Alaya', which translates to 'the abode of Manu'. It is believed that Sage Manu, after the great flood, stepped on this land to recreate human life. This sacred origin gives Manali its spiritual aura and significance.

Historically, the region was inhabited by nomadic tribes. Over centuries, it developed into a strategic trade route connecting the Kullu Valley to Ladakh and Central Asia. The British era saw Manali become a popular summer retreat, and its breathtaking landscapes have since drawn travelers, artists, and adventurers from around the globe. Today, it stands as a cultural hub with ancient temples, serene monasteries, and a rich blend of traditional and modern lifestyles.

Famous Places to Visit

Hadimba Temple

The Hadimba Devi Temple in Manali is a unique 16th-century wooden temple with a pagoda-style roof, dedicated to Hidimba Devi from the Mahabharata epic.

Rohtang Pass

Rohtang Pass is a high-altitude mountain pass near Manali, famous for its breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks, adventurous activities, and as a gateway to the Lahaul and Spiti valleys.

Solang Valley

Solang Valley, located near Manali, is a picturesque destination known for its stunning landscapes and as a year-round hub for adventure sports like paragliding, zorbing, and skiing.

Local Cuisine of Manali

Trout Fish

Freshly caught and cooked trout fish, a local delicacy of Manali. Served with local spices and herbs.

Sidu

Steamed bread made from wheat dough, served with ghee and lentils. Traditional Himachali dish for festivals.

Babru

Fried bread stuffed with black gram paste, a popular Himachali snack. Perfect for tea-time cravings.
